<p><p>The Flame Retardant ABS market is categorized into non-halogen and halogen types based on their fire-resistant properties. Non-halogen flame retardants use safe, environmentally friendly materials that reduce toxic emissions during combustion, making them suitable for various applications, including electronics and automotive sectors. In contrast, halogen-type flame retardants contain bromine or chlorine, providing effective fire resistance but potentially releasing harmful dioxins when burned. Both types serve distinct industries based on fire safety requirements and environmental regulations.</p></p>
