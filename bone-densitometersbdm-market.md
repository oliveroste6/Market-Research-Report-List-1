<p><p>Bone densitometers (BDMs) are specialized medical devices used to measure bone mineral density, helping assess osteoporosis risk. The market is categorized into axial and peripheral BDMs. Axial BDMs, typically found in hospitals, measure bone density in central skeletal sites, such as the spine and hips, providing comprehensive assessments. Peripheral BDMs, on the other hand, are portable devices that assess bone density in peripheral sites like the wrist and heel, offering quick, non-invasive screenings. Both types are essential for early diagnosis and management of bone health.</p></p>
