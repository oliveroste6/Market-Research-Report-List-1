<p><p>The fluorosurfactants market is categorized into four types based on the charge of the molecular structure. Amphoteric fluorosurfactants can function as both acids and bases, offering versatility in various applications. Nonionic fluorosurfactants, lacking a charge, are stable and effective in diverse conditions. Anionic fluorosurfactants carry a negative charge, enhancing wetting and foaming properties. Cationic fluorosurfactants possess a positive charge, providing antimicrobial properties and surface adhesion. These variations cater to specific needs across industries such as coatings, textiles, and oil recovery.</p></p>
