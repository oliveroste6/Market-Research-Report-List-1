<p><p>The Banking and Payment Smart Cards market comprises two primary types: contact-based and contactless smart cards. Contact-based smart cards require physical insertion into a reader for transactions, using metal contacts to transmit data. In contrast, contactless smart cards enable transactions via radio frequency identification (RFID) or near-field communication (NFC), allowing users to tap their card on a reader for quick, secure payments. Both types enhance transaction efficiency, security, and customer convenience in the financial sector.</p></p>
