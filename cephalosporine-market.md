<p><p>Cephalosporins are categorized into four generations, each with distinct properties and market applications. First-generation cephalosporins are effective against Gram-positive bacteria and are commonly used for skin infections. Second-generation agents offer enhanced Gram-negative coverage, making them suitable for respiratory infections. Third-generation cephalosporins provide broad-spectrum activity, targeting more resistant bacteria, often used in serious infections. Fourth-generation cephalosporins are designed for multi-drug resistant organisms, focusing on hospital-acquired infections and surgical prophylaxis, reflecting advancements in antibiotic therapy and evolving market needs.</p></p>
