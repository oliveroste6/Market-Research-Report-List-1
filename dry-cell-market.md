<p><p>The dry cell market is divided into two main types: primary cells and secondary cells. Primary cells are non-rechargeable batteries, designed for single-use applications, commonly found in household devices like flashlights and remote controls. In contrast, secondary cells are rechargeable batteries, capable of multiple charge-discharge cycles, making them ideal for smartphones, laptops, and electric vehicles. Both types are integral to consumer electronics, with primary cells offering convenience and secondary cells promoting sustainability through reuse and prolonged energy storage.</p></p>
