<p><p>The automotive fuel additives market is divided into two main types: gasoline additives and diesel additives. Gasoline additives enhance fuel performance, improve engine efficiency, reduce emissions, and prevent engine knocking, often including detergents and octane boosters. Diesel additives focus on improving cetane rating, enhancing fuel stability, and preventing injector fouling, typically incorporating lubricity agents and cold flow improvers. Both types aim to optimize fuel quality, extend engine life, and comply with environmental regulations while boosting overall vehicle performance.</p></p>
