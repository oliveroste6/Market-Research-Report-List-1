<p><p>The natural food colors market comprises several key types, including caramel, carotenoids, anthocyanins, carmine, and copper chlorophyllin. Caramel is derived from heated sugars, providing a brown hue, while carotenoids, sourced from plants, offer orange to red shades. Anthocyanins, extracted from berries, impart vivid reds and purples. Carmine, made from cochineal insects, delivers red tones, and copper chlorophyllin, a derivative of chlorophyll, provides green shades. These natural colors are preferred for their health benefits and clean-label appeal in food products.</p></p>
